Relationships]

◆ Official Notable Relationships]

  • Simulation Dept.[2]: Kou Ayato 🎞️ & Mao Ukyo 🧩 & Akehoshi Hinomiya ⭐️ & Yuragi Kanno 🍬

They have more opportunities to work together compared to other departments, and they socialize outside of work too, but the sense of unity and camaraderie within their department is weak. Plans for fun activities like "Let's do something with the four of us" from the Simulation Department rarely materialize, but if the four of them do end up going out, they can still enjoy themselves.

  • Business Couple[3]: Kou Ayato 🎞️ and Mao Ukyo 🧩

To avoid trouble, these two have a contract to act as lovers when necessary. It began when Ayato, who has many problems with romantic entanglements, made a request of Ukyo, who accepted it for a fee. In reality, they are just friends, “partners” according to Ayato and “just putting up with each other” according to Ukyo. The incident that led to this partnership is mentioned in Simulation Prequel.

  • Yellow Maker[4]: Kou Ayato 🎞️ and Akehoshi Hinomiya ⭐️

Two people who tend to take on "Yellow" clients — those with warning signs in character acting. They're kindred spirits who share a lot of common ground. They bond over their shared love of movies. While their priorities differ, their tastes in films strangely align, so they often recommend titles to each other.

  • Shukuo Law School[5]: Ai Kosaka 👑 and Kou Ayato 🎞️

Both of them attended Shukuo Law School and were in the same seminar. Kosaka liked how Ayato was neither intimidated by him nor tried to flatter him, and this became the spark for their friendship. Later, Kosaka invited Ayato to join Aporia in order to establish the Simulation Dept..

  • Pastel Prince [6]: Yuzuru Kise 🍴 and Kou Ayato 🎞️

They're classmates and close friends who get along well. Both love ramen and have tried most shops around Shibuya. At their first dress-up event themed around princes, they wore soft-colored outfits as "Spring Prince" and "Autumn Prince," earning them these nicknames from some longtime regulars.

  • TOP2[7]: Kou Ayato 🎞️ and Kyoya Shido 🎨

They are part of Aporia’s “Reboot Group”, the generation with the most members, and became friends through a mutual interest in fashion. Though their preferences differ slightly, their perceptions are similar, and they casually invite each other to go shopping on their days off or for drinks after work. When those two go drinking, it’s almost certain that they’ll be approached by someone.

Gu Niang / クーニャン
Japanese ピーチリキュールをウーロン茶で割ったカクテルクーニャンというのは中国語で「若い娘」を指した言葉。地域によって呼び名が異なっており、その話題でバーテンダーと盛り上がるのも手
English A cocktail made by mixing peach liqueur with oolong tea. The name Gu Niang is a Chinese word that means "young girl." The name varies depending on the region, so chatting about it with the bartender is a good idea.
White Swan* / ホワイト・スワン
Japanese 中華街でデートするなら、やっぱ食べ歩きかな。肉まんとか熱々のものをフーフーする姿って見てて可愛いし、連帯感が生まれない?んで、レストランで辛いものを食べた後に、口直しで杏仁豆腐を頼むところまでが一連の流れ。色んな味を楽しめるのが、醍醐味だよな
English If you're going on a date in Chinatown, you gotta go for the street food. Seeing someone blow on a steaming hot bun or dumpling is just adorable, and it creates this sense of togetherness, right? Then, after eating something spicy at a restaurant, you order almond jelly to cleanse your palate; that's the whole flow. Being able to enjoy all these different flavors is the real treat, isn't it?
Twilight* / 黄昏時
Japanese 今回、一番楽しんでたのは誰かって、間違いなく隠岐谷さんでしょ。シフトの空き時間には中国茶を買いに行ってたとか。まあ、近くに心から楽しんでる人がいるのはいいよな。その方が、なんとなく空気も明るくなるし
English If you ask me who had the most fun this time, it was definitely Okiya-san. I heard he'd go buy Chinese tea during his shift breaks. Well, it's nice having someone nearby who's genuinely having a good time. Somehow, it just makes the whole atmosphere brighter.
Bellini / べリーニ
Japanese ピンクの色合いがチャームポイントのイタリア発フルーツカクテル。可愛らしい見た目かつ甘く飲みやすいためかAporia内で大流行し材料の桃が枯れた時期があったのだとか。
English This Italian fruit cocktail, with its charming pink hue, became hugely popular at Aporia thanks to its cute appearance and sweet, easy-to-drink flavor; there was even a time when the peaches ran out!
Sidecar * / サイドカー
Japanese サイドカーっていうと、俺はカクテルよりバイクの側車の方が浮かぶんだよな。まあでも、人乗せるなら「あ、いるな」って思えて安心するから横じゃなくて後ろかな。
English When I hear "sidecar," I actually think of a motorcycle sidecar rather than a cocktail. But then again, if I were to give someone a ride, I’d feel more at ease knowing, "Oh, they’re there," if they’re behind me rather than beside me.
Pendragon* / ペンドラゴン
Japanese この前ミカ姉が『見た目以上に度数が超高い酒』を教えてくれたんだよね。何のためか聞いたら俺がうっかり飲まされないように、だってさ。まあ確かに、そうやって仕組まれることもこれまでなかったとは言えないからありがたく聞いてきたわ。
English The other day, Mika-nee told me about some "liquor that’s way stronger than it looks." When I asked why, he said it was to make sure I wouldn’t accidentally end up drink it. Well, I guess I can’t say I’ve never been tricked into drinking something like that before, so I was actually grateful he warned me.

A new gaming experience that combines music and match puzzles.

When you "match" pieces of the same color, a seek bar moves in time with the music to erase the pieces!

Aim for a high score with your carefully selected team composition while enjoying the colorful background music that reflects the image of the Aporia staff.

How to Play]

Groove Match Puzzle is a new-style game that combines puzzles and music. Aim for a high score by clearing as many pieces as possible before the song ends.

By clearing puzzles, you can earn user rank EXP and items needed to enhance cards. Additionally, the staff cards in your team will receive Card Lv. EXP and NICE points.

※Insights: Only during Marvellous Challenge, Card Lv. EXP and NICE points aren't added to the team line-up.

※About NICE rewards

If you clear a puzzle with multiple cards of the same staff in your team, you will receive NICE points for each card of that staff. On the result screen, the number of NICE earned by each staff member during that play will be shown per card.

※Insights: To raise the NICE points fast, you can set up the team line-up of just one staff.

◆ Unit ]

When you swap pieces so that three or more pieces of the same color line up, you create a unit.

◆ Seek Bar ]

If the unit touches the seek bar that moves with the music, the unit will be clear.

※Insights: See more Seek Bar to know more types of seek bar.

◆ Combo Piece ]

When you clear a unit made of four or more pieces, a Combo Piece will appear. Combo Pieces are erased simply by touching the seek bar.

◆ Combo ]

The number of units and Combo Pieces you erase while the seek bar moves once from one end of the board to the other is called a combo. Stacking combos lets you earn higher scores.

※ Insights: The number of combos created by erasing the combo piece is larger than erasing a three-piece unit. When you form the combo piece, you get 1 combo, and additional combos in the next erasing.

◆ SP Skill ]

Main article: Cards' SP skill

The SP skill gauge increases according to the number of combos.

When the gauge reaches MAX, try tapping the glowing gauge. You can activate a powerful SP Skill.

The effect of the SP Skill varies depending on the card. The SP Skill of the card set as the leader will activate.

◆ Auto Skill ]

Main article: Cards' Auto skill

Auto skill is an effect that activates automatically during play. When you clear pieces that match the card’s attribute, the gauge fills.

※ Support cards’ auto skills are not counted as part of your formation.

◆ Score ]

Clearing pieces increases your score. The higher the overall power of the cards in your team, the higher a score you can aim for.

If you include cards that match the stage’s attribute or strengthen card levels, your overall power will increase.

※ Insights: attribute boost is 19.90% to the overall power.

◆ Life ]

If no combo is achieved while the seek bar moves from one end to the other, you take damage, and your Life decreases.

※The number of Lives varies by stage, but in general, the maximum number is 5.

If your Life reaches 0, it’s game over.

◆ Target ]

Some stages have targets set. If you fail to clear the targets before the song ends, it’s game over.

You can check the remaining time of the song on the progress bar at the bottom of the screen.

※ About stages where normal pieces are set as targets If a specific color of a normal piece is set as a target, then clearing a same-colored Hirameki piece or Bomb piece does not count toward the target.

Moreover, when you swipe a Hirameki piece or Bomb piece with a rainbow piece, the same-colored pieces will then change to Hirameki Pieces or Bomb Pieces before being erased by the effect. However, such pieces that were converted (the Hirameki/Bomb pieces) are also not counted toward the target in that case.

◆ Auto/Loop Function ]

[Auto Function]

Tap the [AUTO] button shown at the bottom right of the screen during puzzle play to enable auto mode; pieces will be matched automatically. Tap the [AUTO] button again during autoplay to cancel autoplay. Please note that autoplay may still result in a game over in some cases.

※ During autoplay, SP Skills will activate automatically.
※ Autoplay may not achieve targets or may allow Life to decrease, so use with caution.

[Loop Function]

If you tap anywhere on the screen during loop play, a confirmation pop-up will appear, and you can stop the loop. Loop play will also be automatically interrupted under the following conditions:

  • You don't have enough stamina.
  • During play, the date changes, maintenance begins, additional data needs to be downloaded,

or any other situation requires returning to the title screen.

  • You get a game over during loop play.

※ For loop play, support cards are automatically redrawn for every run from the second play onward.

Puzzle Setting]

You can change puzzle-related settings on the Puzzle Settings screen. While playing a puzzle or from the global menu, open [Settings] to access:

  • Homescreen > [MENU] > [Settings] > [PUZZLE]
  • [Settings] in the team setup screen

◆ Clear sound settings]

You can change the sound played when a unit is cleared.

There are 3 sounds: PIANO, MARIMBA, and NEON. You can check the preview sound by pressing the nearby button.

◆ Volume settings]

You can adjust the volume of BGM, sound effects, and voices during puzzle play. There is also [Reset to Default] (デフォルトに戻す) button.

◆ Support]

Automatically select support cards during play from [Play Again] on the result screen. ※ When OFF, you can manually reselect support cards.

◆ SP Skill ]

[Auto Activate]
When turned ON, SP skills will activate automatically.
[Do not show cut-in after the 2nd time]
Cut-in animations for skill activation will not be shown from the second activation onward.

◆ Vibration ]

[SP Skill]
When the SP skill gauge reaches MAX, vibration will activate.
[Target]
Vibration activates when a target is achieved.

◆ Color settings ]

You can darken the colors of the pieces.

[Darken Color]
When ON, the colors of the pieces become darker.

◆ Effects ]

[Special Effect]
When ON, special effects will be shown when a high score is updated.

Playlist]

Tap the [PUZZLE] button on the home screen to display a list of playlists. You can switch the selected playlist by tapping the icon area at the bottom of the screen or by swiping across the screen. Tap the department's name to jump to that department. Tap the [NEXT] button at the bottom left of the screen to view a list of songs within the selected playlist.

◆ About staff-only playlists]

You must include a staff member’s card from the selected playlist as the leader.

◆ Total High Score]

The total high score is displayed. It is the sum of the high scores from all stages in each playlist.

Stages]

When you select a playlist, the playable stages will be shown in a list.

◆ SIDE ]

There are three sides: SIDE A, SIDE B, and hidden SIDE C. Changing the SIDE will alter the stage contents. ※Rewards and scores are recorded separately for each SIDE.

Further information: § Unlock Conditions

◈ SIDE A ]

[SIDE A]
Preceding stages must be cleared, except for some stages.

◈ SIDE B]

[SIDE B]
Most of Side B will be unlocked if the score at SIDE A is A and above, except for Personal Song.

◈ SIDE C]

[SIDE C]
This stage is unlocked once all achievement boards on both sides A & B are cleared.

◆ Stage Attributes ]

  • Each stage has an attribute.
  • If you include cards with the same attribute as the stage in your team, a bonus will be added to your overall power.
  • Attributes are Sun, Moon, Star, and All-attribute. For All-attribute stages, cards of any attribute will count toward the bonus.

◆ Unlock conditions ]

Some stages have unlock conditions. When you meet the unlock conditions, the stage becomes playable.

Support Card]

When playing puzzles, you need to include support cards in addition to your own cards. When you select a stage within a playlist, cards set by other users as supports will be displayed. Selecting a card takes you to the team setup screen.

Support cards provide the following effects:

※Auto skills of support cards are not included in the effects they provide.

[Overall Power]
The team’s overall power includes the overall power of the support card.
[Leader Effect]
In addition to the leader effect of the card you set as leader, the leader effects of support cards also activate.
[Combination Effect]
SP Skill combinations apply to combination partners that include support cards.
[Event Bonus]
Event bonuses also apply to support cards; they will be active even if the card is a support.

◆ Support Points ]

When you include support cards and clear puzzles, you earn Support Points (in short Support pt). If you select a user you follow as your support, you (and your following) receive more Support pt than usual (15pt vs 10pt). In other words, you also receive support pt, if other players use your card; for non-followers, it is 10pt and for your followers, it is 15pt.

※Support pts are granted on next day logins according to the number of times your card was used.

You can exchange earned Support pt for items at the Support Point Exchange.

Team Setup]

On the team setup screen, you arrange the cards you’ll use to play puzzles. Tap the four non-support slots and set five cards to start a puzzle. Set a leader in the slot labeled [LEADER].
※Note: You can only equip staff that matches the currently selected playlist.

◆ Auto‑arranged team ]

Tap the [Auto-arranged Team] button to have cards placed automatically. ※Note: It's usually picked from the cards having the top overall power, though.

◆ Team management ]

You can check the possible combination of your current formation. Tap the [Combination] button to see. You can save up to three team formations per playlist, and can rename them within 4 letters. After version 1.4.99 (?), the number of team formations is increased to five. Tap the [Team Management] button to open the team management screen. ※Note: After version 1.4.99 (?), the [Team Management] button is gone; you can only navigate the five team formations by tapping the left or right buttons.

◆ Costume change ]

Tap the [Style] icon to the right of the [Combination] button to change the leader staff’s costume. ※Note: Default Live 2d custumes and costumes of the cards that have been revised to revision 1 are displayed here.

◆ Puzzle Setting]

You can access the puzzle setting and other settings within the team setup screen.

◆ Power Preview]

There is a switch icon where you can switch to three modes to see: Leader effect, Support effect, and Auto skill of four cards in the formation. Moreover, the list of effects (Leader, Auto, and SP Skill) can also be accessed in the pause screen within the puzzle game.

The [Overall Power] will show the calculated power of your formation:

  • Card Overall Power (カード総合力, Kādo sōgōryoku?): Total of all the cards includes your four cards and support cards by:
  • Aura:
  • Visual:

  • Stage Bonus: if the card matches the puzzle stage's attribute, it will add 19.99% of such card's overall power.
  • Leader Bonus: the sum of the leader bonus from the leader card and the support card
  • Combination: the sum of all the available combinations within the team formation.

The score rank evaluator will show the expected score rank that the current formation can achieve. ※Note: This score rank is just for your reference, it won't reflect your final score in the puzzle all the time. You can perform better or worse than expected, though.

※Insights 1: How the Power Preview is calculated?

※Insights 2: Raising Overall Power To raise the overall power is best to:

  • Have many SSR cards, the max out status card is a bonus: Of course, SSR cards have the biggest overall power, where max out cards have the highest power.
  • Match the effect of the leader card and the support card. If the card has an X leader effect, the same X effect also applies when it becomes a support card.

Example 1: your leader effect is "Aura of Team is 50% UP!" then it's preferable to choose another support card with the same "Aura of Team is 50% UP!". Then you will have the 100% boost to the Aura element of the whole team.

  • Has as many combinations as possible in the team formation (maximum 5): For SSR cards, the combination's effect is 10% max, while SR cards are 6% max.
  • For Aura/Visual/Charisma boost, the cards that have the dominating power of such elements are preferable: The dominating power simply means that an element has the highest number compared to the other two, except for Balance Power, where all power elements are equal. Their leader skills are also an overall power boost, though.

Also, example 1: cards that have Aura dominating power will enjoy the boost better than other dominating elements.

  • Applied rules: If the stage has rules, the icon [Rule] will appear right next to the color of the pieces appearing.

◆ Boost items ]

If you own boost items that grant special effects in puzzles, you can use them by tapping the button shown in the [BOOST] area at the bottom of the screen. There are three types of boost items:

[Coin Boost]
Increases coins earned from the puzzle when used.
[Level Boost]
Increases card EXP earned from the puzzle when used.
[Drop Boost]
Increases the number of item drops from the puzzle when used.

◆ Stamina boost ]

Main article: Stamina Boost

Tap the [Settings] button next to the Start button to change the amount of Stamina consumed per play.

In a specific staff playlist, the maximum stamina boost is 3. Only in the OJT event, the maximum stamina boost is 10.

Tap the [Skip] button next to the Start button to skip-clear a stage.

Skip Ticket]

Using a Skip Ticket lets you skip a normal puzzle stage and receive its rewards.

  • You cannot skip stages you have not cleared.
  • Skipping a puzzle consumes Stamina just like normal play.

◆ Skip Ticket replenishment]

You can replenish up to 1 Skip Ticket per day. If you don't use a replenished Skip Ticket, it will not be replenished the next day.

※ Consumed Skip Tickets are replenished daily at 4:00 AM.

◆ Team composition when skipping ]

When you press the [Clear Now] button, the cards that are set on the team screen will receive Card Lv. EXP and Nice.

Background Play]

◆ How to Background Play]

When you use Background Play, puzzle stages can be cleared automatically even if the app is in the background or closed.

※ Background Play is available only for SIDE-A stages that have been cleared in the normal playlist. and for stages in limited-event playlists of the "Cafebar" format.

To use Background Play, open the Background Play settings from the [Proxy] (代行, Dairi) button on the team setup screen

before playing a puzzle stage, set the number of clears, and tap the [Execute] button.

When Background Play finishes, you can check the results.

  • Rewards, various experience gains, and mission clear counts are all processed together when you view the results.
  • Card Lv. EXP and Nice will be granted to the cards that were set on the team screen when Background Play was started.

To stop Background Play, tap the [Stop Background] button on the song selection screen.

  • Items and experience from cleared runs will still be granted.
  • Unused Stamina will be returned.

※ Note: that Stamina returned will not exceed your possession cap, so any Stamina that would push you over the limit will not be returned.

※ Insights: There are 3 limits: 20, 40, and 99 stamina.

  • 20 stamina: When the stamina drops lower than 20, new stamina will be refilled in 10 minutes, and it will stop when reaching 20.
  • 40 stamina: You can use stamina charge, crystal, or other stamina consumable items to raise your stamina to 40. Stamina ranging from 21 to 40 won't be automatically refilled when it drops until the 20 limit hits.
  • 99 stamina: When you're a new player, the EXP for each user rank is small, and each user rank upgrade will reward you with an extra 20 stamina and other unlocks (Main Story, follower limit, etc.) Then, you will upgrade fast with lots of stamina in hand, but it will never surpass 99 stamina. Once you almost reach a new user rank, it's best to use up more stamina than usual, so the stamina won't go to waste, such as using double/triple stamina in a puzzle.

◆ Additional notes about Background Play ]

  • The score recorded for clears during Background Play is a fixed value determined by the team you set.
  • Clears via Background Play require the same amount of time as an actual playthrough.
  • Background Play consumes Stamina just like normal play.
  • It cannot be used on event playlists or Marverlous Challenge stages.
  • Results from Background Play do not count toward the achievement conditions listed in a puzzle’s "Completion Rewards."
  • Some missions (e.g. Main Mission and Achievement) will not progress when using Background Play.

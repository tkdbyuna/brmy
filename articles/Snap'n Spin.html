Snap'n Spin

Snap'n Spin is a snapshot mini-game that lets you raise a card’s Story Level. Also, you can learn more about characters through snap titles and comments.

How to Play]

Navigation: On the Home screen, choose [SPIN] > [START]

◆ SPIN]

Stop the rotating film at your preferred timing! Go take a snap of the chosen staff member.

One spin section has 6 spins and costs 1 stamina. On the left corner, you can see the number of spins in the section and items that have been dropped so far.

In general, one spin section rewards 300 coins and a 20 EXP raise in User Rank. If your user rank is maximum, the rank reward will be awarded instead.

For staff, staff in the line-up get 25 EXP as the base, regardless of appearing at the photo spot or not. If Story Lv. is max, EXP won't be added anymore. See the summary in Table 1.1

◈ SPIN Mode]

There are three spin modes, represented by a row of buttons on the top right corner:

  • [AUTO]: The game will roll out the staff automatically. During the section, you can only take a snap at a photo spot. Once it finishes one section, the spin will stop.
  • [LOOP]: The same as auto, yet the spin will loop multiple sections instead of once, until some special case forces the loop to stop:
  • Stamina runs out
  • Game updates that need the game to return to the Title Screen
  • Some cards successfully max a Story Lv.
  • [SPEED x2]: Fasten the speed of spins 2 times

There is also a [PAUSE] button to pause the spin or stop the Auto/Loop mode. You cannot opt out of a running spin section, except for the game bug or a time-out.

◈ FEVER ]

Also, on the left corner, the FEVER gauge bar accumulates every time EXP is collected. Once it's full, the spins will be strengthened, and you can earn double Story Lv. EXP, higher drop rate, and higher chance to meet the rare spot.

A FEVER section contains 4 spins; in some cases, you can even encounter consecutive FEVER (the screen will display [FEVER CONTINUE]).

◈ Photo Spot ]

At the photo spot, the selected staff will earn Story Lv. EXP.

You may receive drop items used to enhance Story Lv., such as [Disk], and NICE points for such staff. Sometimes, other card enhancement items, such as File Request, Cocktail, Small Essence, etc., can appear.

If a gold or rainbow film is selected, you can go to the rare photo spot. At these rare photo spots, you can see other staff members as well and earn even more Story Lv. EXP. In some cases, you can encounter a rotating film that guarantees a rare photo spot and above.

So far, there are 4 photo spots available:

  • Space Casino: A solo spot
  • American Diner: A new solo spot which is introduced in the events Coming Up! Assorted Special Menu.
  • Cartoon Factory: A rare spot where two staff members appear
  • Sweets Land: A super rare spot where the department/trio/quartet appears.

★ Tips: Special cases when staff wear a specific mini style
  • 2nd Birthday mini Styles: enable the chance to go to a special Space Casino and get a 2nd birthday snap
  • 3rd Birthday mini Styles: enable the chance to go to a special Sweets Land and get a 3rd birthday snap, when other fellow dept. member comes to celebrate. This doesn't require the whole department staff in the spin line-up.

Tap the photo button at the bottom left to take a snap.

When you check the snaps you took in the album feature, some will have special comments attached.

◆ SNAP ]

Navigation: On the Home screen, choose [SPIN] > [SNAP]

[SNAP] is where you access all the snaps taken during SPIN.

◈ KEEP ]

[KEEP]
Snaps you take are temporarily saved to [KEEP].

You can temporarily store up to 60 snaps.

※Note: If more than 60 snaps are saved in KEEP, the oldest ones will be deleted first.

If you want to store them permanently, you need to move them to [ALBUM].

You can move snaps selected from [KEEP] into the Album. Choose the snaps you want to keep and save them to the Album.

◈ ALBUM ]

[ALBUM]
In the Album feature, you can use snaps taken in Spin to create your own personal album.


Snaps moved from [KEEP] are shown in a list in the album. Tap the album’s [Sort] (並び替え, narabikae?, lit. rearrange) button to rearrange snaps by sliding them. Tap the [OK] button to save the order.

By default, there are two albums, which can contain up to 20 snaps each.

You can:

  • Change the album's name (アルバム名設定, arubamu-mei settei?, lit. Album Name Setting), within 10 Japanese characters.
  • Share or delete.
  • Favorite one snap only, and it will feature the album cover. You set no snap for favorites; then the album cover will be blank. If you want to change the favorite snap, simply tap [♡] for another snap.
  • [TAG OFF] button: Switch tag on/off for SR and SSR snaps, you can share either tag-on or tag-off snaps.
  • [Move to Another Album] button (別のアルバムへ移動, betsu no arubamu e idō?, lit. Move to Another Album): move snaps to another album.

You can raise the album's slots using Free Crystal by tapping the [Expansion] button (拡張, Kakuchō?, lit. Expansion). Five slots are equivalent to 10 Crystals, and you can raise capacity to 50 slots per album.

Once you reach the maximum slots, if you want to expand more, you can buy 1 more album at Crystal Exchange Shop. From 2nd anniversary, the number of albums can be bought is increased to two.

★ Tips: : Color code for snaps The snap's rarity and where it was taken can be easily detected:

  • Snaps have three rarities: R, SR, and SSR, and each spot will have a separate set of rarities.
  • At the [SPIN RESULT] screen, you will see DROP ITEM, USER RANK, and SNAP sections.

The snaps here display as a thumbnail with a border, whose color follows the same color rule as the card rarity. However, the thumbnail doesn't show the spot icon, so you can only know by tapping to view it.

  • In [KEEP] or [ALBUM], the snaps display as a polaroid picture.
  • R snaps have a white Polaroid frame.
  • SR snaps have a colored Polaroid frame, and a tiny icon at the top left corner shows the snapping locations:

Team Setup]

You can start a Spin once you have a team of 5 cards. In a Spin, you must include cards of five different staff members.

※Note: You cannot include multiple cards of the same staff at the same time.

There is a recommend button (おまかせ編成, omakase hensei?, lit. Auto Formation or Recommended Setup), so the game can set the team for you.

[CARD SELECT]
Only cards having Card Story will be displayed here (which means R, SR, and SSR).

◆ Change Mini Style ]

Tap the button shown at the bottom right of each team slot to change the mini style of the staff in that slot.

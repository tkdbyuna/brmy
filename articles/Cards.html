Cards are central mechanic in Break My Case. Using cards is to clear the puzzles, earn card enhancement items, raise the card level as well as their characters' NICE Points.

Cards can be earned from Gacha and Events's rewards. They have four rarities and three power attributes. Its overall power includes three elements: Aura, Visual, and Charisma.

Staff List]

You can view a list of the cards you own. Tapping a card icon opens the card enhancement screen.

◆ Waiting Deck]

If you obtain multiple copies of the same card, the second and subsequent copies will be sent to the Waiting Deck.

◆ Sort & Filter]

You can sort or filter the cards in the list. Using [Free-text Search], you can search for cards by entering words found in staff names, card names, or skill descriptions.

Staff Enhancement]

You can use items to enhance your cards. The items needed to enhance cards can be obtained through puzzles, spins, and the exchange shop.

Card Status]

On the [Status] (ステータス, sutētasu?) tab, you can check the current status of your cards. To view the status of the card, players can either tap or press and hold this button.

By switching tabs, you can strengthen each of these categories:

  • Card Lv.
  • Story Lv.
  • Skill
  • Revision
  • Limit Break

As the Base Status, cards have:

  • Card Lv.: Lv.1
  • Story Lv.: Lv.0
  • Auto Skill Lv.: Lv.1
  • SP Skill Lv.: Lv.1
  • Leader Skill Lv. (if any): Lv.1
  • Combination Lv. (if any): Lv.1
  • Revision Rank: 0
  • Cap Level: 1

◆ Card Rarity]

Initially, there are three rarities: SSR, SR, and R. Over time, two new rarities are added:

  • XR: Extremely Rare(?) is introduced from 0.5th Anniversary.
  • CR: Combi Rare is introduced in the event Vignette -Emperor-Skip-.

◆ Card Attributes]

Each staff card has its own attribute.

If you include cards with the same attribute as a stage, a bonus will be added to your overall power, and if you include them in a stage where pieces of the same attribute appear, you can activate Auto Skills.

◆ Combination]

Except for R cards, cards have combination partners. Long-press the staff icon in the bottom-right corner of the Status screen to view the combination effects.

Card Lv. can be raised by playing puzzle or using the file requests (gained from playing puzzle or exchange in CHANGE shop) and Coins:

In the [Card Lv.] tab, you can level up your cards. As your Card Lv. increases, the card's overall power will increase.

Story Level]

Strengthening the Story Lv. (ストーリーLv., sutoorii reberu?, lit. Story Level, in short Story Lv.) unlocks the Card Story.

A card’s Story Lv. can be raised by playing Snap'n Spin or using the item Disc (gained from playing Snap'n Spin or exchanged in CHANGE shop) and coins:

In the [Story Lv.] tab, you can level up your Story Lv. Card stories are unlocked based on the card's Story Lv.

Depending on the Cards' rarity, the number of unlocked card's story varies:

  • SSR: three-episode Card Story
  • SR: two-episode Card Story
  • R: one-episode Card Story
  • XR: no Card Story

Skills]

Skills have a wide range of effects in puzzles. In the [Skill] tab, you can level up your skills. To see how to obtain the items needed to level up a skill, tap the item icon. Higher levels will require more items and coins to upgrade.

◆ Leader Skill]

Main article: Leader Skill

Except for R Cards, the Leader card (in-game display as LEADER) in the team line-up will have the leader skill's boost. The R Cards will show "NO SKILL" for the leader skill.

The leader skill will boost one element of overall power (Aura, Visual, or Charisma) or the overall power of the cards in the team. Some special cards will boost the power of the Department's staff or specific staff only.

See also: Groove Match Puzzle § Auto Skill

◆ SP Skill]

See also: Groove Match Puzzle § SP Skill

Raising the SP Skill Lv. (SPスキルLv., SP sukiru reberu?, lit. SP Skill Lv.) makes the skill’s effect stronger.

By building up the SP Skill gauge through combos during gameplay, you can activate and unleash it manually or automatically.

Only the SP Skill of the Leader card is used in the puzzle game.

By default, when the SP gauge is full, players need to tap it to unleash the skill, and it will cost a certain amount of SP. However, players can change the settings to make it automatically unleash when the gauge is full. You can read more about it in puzzle settings.

New effects will be added over time. Currently, SP Skill has 6 main types:

  • Change Skill
  • Erase Skill
  • Gimmick Skill
  • Make Skill
  • Other Skill
  • Recover Skill

and 61 sub-skills:

◆ Combination]

This takes effect when a Combination partner is in your team. Raising the Combination Lv. makes the effect more powerful.

When selecting the team of five cards, players will choose four of their own and the fifth card from another player as the support card. The combination card will boost the dominant power element of its partnered card in the team.

Combination Lv. can be enhanced by Item (gained from playing puzzles or exchanging in the CHANGE shop) and coins.

Combination Lv. only has 3 levels:

  • Lv.1 has a 3% boost
  • Lv.2 has a 6% boost
  • Lv.3 has a 10% boost

From Version [Verification needed], you can check the possible combinations available in your current team line-up.

Depending on rarity, the Combination level varies and requires conditions. See Revision for more details.

◆ SP Skill Combination ]

Only the SSR Cards, aside from the power element boost, the combination card will have the additional SP skill. The SP skill combination is unlocked when the leader card's SP Skill is at Lv. 5, and will activate right after the leader card's SP skill activation.

Not all the SP Skills can be used as the SP Skill Combination, the most common ones are:

  1. Erase Piece Random
  2. Erase Pieces by Attribute group
  3. Change Pieces by Attribute group
  4. Make Combo R (or Single Combo Piece)
  5. Make Bomb, Bomb Cracker, and Make Bomb Piece
  6. Make Hirameki, Hirameki Cracker, and Make Hirameki Piece
  7. Make Special Piece

Please note that the numbers 2 & 3 are a group of SP Skills, while the rest are the specific SP skills.

Revision]

Performing a Revision (リビジョン, ribijon?, lit. Revision) not only increases the card's status level, but also unlocks card-exclusive content such as pins and styles.

On the [Revision] tab, you can revise your cards. To see how to obtain the items required for revision, tap the item icon.

For SP Skill and Auto Skill, raising the cards from Lv.1 to Lv.2 won't require anything except the card enhancement items and coins.

◆ Live Clip]

When you enhance an SSR card to Revision Rank 3, you unlock a Live Clip, which features an animated card illustration. You can set Live Clips as your home screen or profile background to enjoy them.

Waiting Deck]

If you obtain multiple copies of the same card, the second and subsequent copies will be sent to the waiting deck. Cards in the waiting deck can also be converted into pink feathers.

※Cards exchanged for pink feathers cannot be reverted.

Pink Feather]

This item can be obtained by converting cards in the waiting room. Pink Feathers can be exchanged for other items at the Exchange Shop.

※Gacha points that have expired will also be converted into Pink Feathers.
